💬 Customer Sentiment Analysis — Top Review Themes (Q2 2026)

👍 What Customers Praise (GCC-wide)

  • Speed of motor quote & buy — top positive theme across all markets (42% of positive reviews)
  • Cashless health claims — Bupa Arabia + Daman cited most; reduces friction to zero
  • Arabic app quality — Tawuniya praised specifically for native Arabic UX depth
  • Real-time claims tracking — Tawuniya + QIC; customers value transparency above payout speed
  • Digital renewal reminders — WhatsApp + push notifications praised when implemented
  • Telematics rewards — Tawuniya Drive loyalty points cited as NPS booster

👎 What Customers Criticise (GCC-wide)

  • Digital start → offline finish — #1 complaint GCC-wide; online quote but branch sign-off required
  • Claims payout delays — even when FNOL is digital, settlement reverts to manual process
  • AI chatbot uselessness — rule-based bots cited as “worse than nothing” in 28% of negative reviews
  • Arabic UX quality — translated-not-localised; RTL bugs; Arabic content incomplete
  • App instability — crashes during payment, OTP failures, session timeouts common
  • No self-service endorsements — address change, vehicle change require branch visit

📊 Complaint Volume Breakdown (Regulated Markets)

  • Claims-related: 54% of all complaints — payout delay + process complexity
  • Policy servicing: 22% — renewal, endorsement, certificate issues
  • Sales & mis-selling: 12% — product mismatch, Arabic/English policy wording gaps
  • App/portal issues: 8% — technical failures during purchase or claims
  • Billing: 4% — double-charge, refund delays
  • KSA IA + UAE IA publish complaint ratios; QCB + CBK data less transparent

CX insight: The GCC insurance CX gap is not a product gap — it is a journey completion gap. Carriers that start digitally but force customers offline to complete claims, endorsements, or renewals are destroying NPS at the finish line. Tawuniya and Bupa Arabia prove that a GCC insurer can achieve global-benchmark CX (+62 NPS, 4.6★ app, 81% claims CSAT). The delta between best and worst in GCC is 66 NPS points — the largest competitive gap in any dimension measured in this dashboard.
