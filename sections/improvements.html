🔧 Improvements Required Across GCC Insurance

Based on the Roland Berger Digital Insurance Trends 2025 GCC study, Swiss Re and Alpen Capital 2026 analyses, six strategic clusters of improvement are critical for GCC insurers to remain competitive and capture the digital growth opportunity.

1️⃣ Core System Modernisation

  • 🔴 Replace legacy monolithic policy admin systems (most carriers 15–25 yr old platforms)
  • 🔴 Migrate to cloud-native, API-first insurance cores (Guidewire Cloud, Majesco Cloud, Sapiens)
  • 🟠 Implement microservices architecture for independent product/channel scaling
  • 🟠 Decouple distribution from policy admin to enable omni-channel flexibility
  • 🟡 Adopt real-time data streaming (Kafka/Kinesis) for live risk and pricing signals
  • 🟡 Implement event-driven architecture for claims automation

2️⃣ Customer Experience Overhaul

  • 🔴 Fully self-serve online journey: quote → bind → pay → certificate in under 3 minutes
  • 🔴 End-to-end digital claims: FNOL online, photo/video upload, real-time status tracking
  • 🟠 Arabic-first UX with RTL-optimised design and Arabic NLP search
  • 🟠 WhatsApp-native journeys (GCC's primary messaging channel for commerce)
  • 🟠 Mobile-first app with sub-2s load, biometric login, in-app policy management
  • 🟡 Proactive notifications: renewal alerts, claim updates, health reminders via push
  • 🟡 Live chat + AI chatbot with human escalation for complex queries

3️⃣ Product Innovation

  • 🔴 Launch retail cyber insurance (SME + individual) — zero competition currently
  • 🔴 Build wellness-reward health products (Vitality/AIA model) for GCC
  • 🔴 Introduce parametric travel: auto-payout on flight delay, cancellation
  • 🟠 Develop income protection and critical illness as standalone digital products
  • 🟠 Create SME packaged cover (property + liability + cyber) online in <5 minutes
  • 🟠 On-demand/daily motor cover for weekend drivers, tourists
  • 🟡 Family Takaful with digital savings dashboard
  • 🟡 Pet insurance as a digital-native product for UAE/KSA pet owners

4️⃣ Data & AI Strategy

  • 🔴 Build unified customer data platform (CDP) to consolidate siloed data sources
  • 🔴 Deploy AI-powered underwriting: risk scoring using alternative data, not just credit
  • 🟠 Computer vision for motor damage assessment — reduce claims settlement to <48hrs
  • 🟠 Fraud detection ML models — estimated 15–20% of GCC motor claims fraudulent
  • 🟠 NLP-powered claims processing — auto-extract documents, classify claims
  • 🟠 Predictive renewal: ML model for churn risk, personalised retention offers
  • 🟡 Generative AI for policy Q&A, customer service bot, agent assist
  • 🟡 Arabic LLM fine-tuning for GCC-specific insurance content generation

5️⃣ Distribution & Ecosystem

  • 🔴 Build open API layer for embedded insurance partnerships (banks, fintechs, e-commerce)
  • 🔴 Publish developer portal with sandbox for partners to embed insurance products
  • 🟠 Establish super-app presence: Al Rajhi, STC Pay, OPay, Careem integrations
  • 🟠 Deploy white-label insurance for banking partners (BaaS → IaaS model)
  • 🟠 Aggregator API partnerships with Tameeni, yallacompare, souqalmal for data advantage
  • 🟡 Insurtech accelerator / co-innovation programmes to source proprietary AI

6️⃣ Talent & Organisation

  • 🔴 Hire or retrain: data scientists, ML engineers, product managers with digital background
  • 🔴 Form cross-functional squads (product + tech + actuarial) for agile delivery
  • 🟠 Invest in Arabic AI/NLP expertise — severely scarce in GCC insurance
  • 🟠 Partner with universities (KAUST, NYU Abu Dhabi, CMU Qatar) for AI talent pipeline
  • 🟡 Build internal design capability: UX/UI designers who understand Arabic-first design
  • 🟡 Digital leadership: CDO or Chief Digital Officer empowered at board level
Priority framework: 🔴 Critical (deploy 2026) · 🟠 High (roadmap 2026–2027) · 🟡 Medium (2027–2028). Roland Berger's GCC study confirms core system replacement and AI are the top two priorities cited by GCC insurance CEOs and CIOs in 2025.
