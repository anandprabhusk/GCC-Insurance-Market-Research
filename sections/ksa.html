🇰🇪 Saudi Arabia — Insurance Market 2026

Saudi Arabia is the GCC's dominant insurance market, with insurance revenue reaching SAR 71.2B (~$19B) in 2025 — over 39% of the entire GCC total — growing at 7.3% YoY (Milliman KSA Year-End 2025) driven by mandatory health (cooperative insurance) and motor schemes, Vision 2030 infrastructure projects, and rising SME formation. The Insurance Authority (IA), formerly SAMA's insurance arm, regulates 29 licensed insurers under the Cooperative Insurance Law. Tawuniya leads digital transformation with the region's most advanced insurer app; Bupa Arabia dominates health. Yet cyber, life, and SME remain almost entirely offline even in this most mature of GCC markets.

📈 KSA Market Intelligence — Unique Factors

🏛 Vision 2030 Insurance Drivers

  • NEOM & Giga Projects: $500B+ construction requiring specialist insurance — currently 100% broker/offline
  • SME target: IA mandating SME insurance packages — digital delivery gap is policy priority
  • Tourism: Saudi e-visa + tourism targets 150M visitors by 2030 — travel insurance demand surge
  • Housing: 500K homes target — property/mortgage insurance entirely underdeveloped digitally
  • Sport & Events: Formula E, Dakar Rally, LIV Golf — event/liability insurance gap

🛡 Cyber Insurance: The $0 Market

  • NCA Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC) mandate for all entities — creates legal cyber insurance demand
  • PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law) fines up to SAR 5M — D&O and cyber liability need
  • Zero KSA insurers have retail cyber product online — first mover takes entire segment
  • Estimated KSA cyber GWP potential: SAR 2.4B ($640M) by 2028
  • International players (Chubb, AIG) only serving large corporates via brokers — SME cyber is open

⚖ Regulatory Catalysts

  • IA Open Insurance Framework: Consultation underway — API-based policy data sharing expected 2026–2027
  • SAMA Fintech Lab: Insurance sandbox graduates expected to challenge incumbents 2026
  • Tameeni mandate: SAMA requires all motor policies issued via Tameeni — commoditises motor
  • CCHI health mandates: Expanding mandatory health to dependants — 3M+ new lives to insure
  • NCA ECC: Cybersecurity compliance forces cyber risk awareness — insurance demand follows

KSA market insight: Saudi Arabia is the GCC's most digitally advanced insurance market yet paradoxically has the most untapped whitespace in high-value lines. Insurance revenue reached SAR 71.2B (~$19B) in 2025 (Milliman), growing at 7.3% YoY. Tawuniya and Bupa Arabia set the regional digital benchmark, but the entire market has zero cyber, minimal SME, and near-zero embedded insurance — precisely the segments that Vision 2030's economic diversification agenda will drive demand for. The first carrier to launch a fully digital cyber + SME bundle will capture an estimated SAR 3B+ in addressable premium with almost no competition.
