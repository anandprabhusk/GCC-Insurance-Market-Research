🇦🇪 Saudi Arabia — Insurance Market 2026
📋 Source note: KSA market size from Milliman KSA Insurance Industry Year-End 2025 Monitor (SAR 71.2B revenue, +7.3% YoY). GCC total from Alpen Capital GCC Insurance Industry Report, May 2026 ($48.5B GWP). Digital maturity and digital channel share are internal estimates based on product coverage and UX audits. Regulator: Insurance Authority (IA), formerly SAMA’s insurance arm, under Cooperative Insurance Law.

Saudi Arabia is the GCC’s dominant insurance market, with insurance revenue reaching SAR 71.2B (~$19B) in 2025 — over 39% of the entire GCC total — growing at 7.3% YoY (Milliman KSA Year-End 2025) driven by mandatory health (cooperative insurance) and motor schemes, Vision 2030 infrastructure projects, and rising SME formation. The Insurance Authority (IA) regulates 29 licensed insurers under the Cooperative Insurance Law. Tawuniya leads digital transformation with the region’s most advanced insurer app; Bupa Arabia dominates health. Yet cyber, life, and SME remain almost entirely offline even in this most mature of GCC markets.

🎗 Digital Native  Score 8.5–10

Born digital. 100% online purchase + claims across all lines. AI-driven underwriting, embedded APIs, mobile-first. Tawuniya (8.9) is KSA’s closest carrier to this tier, with telematics UBI live in production.

🎉 Digital Leader  Score 7–8.5

Broad online portfolio (3+ lines), native app rated 4+, digital claims with live tracking, self-service portal, at least basic AI. Bupa Arabia (8.7), ACIG (7.8), Al Rajhi Takaful (7.4) sit in this band.

📈 Digital Liner  Score 5–7

1–2 lines buyable online, limited app, partial claims. Medgulf (6.8), Walaa (5.9), Salama (5.4) occupy this range.

🔶 Basic Online  Score 3–5

Motor-quote-only site, no meaningful self-service, no app or a thin wrapper. Several KSA smaller cooperative carriers fall here, mostly accessible only via Tameeni aggregator.

📈 KSA Market Intelligence — Unique Factors

🏛 Vision 2030 Insurance Drivers

  • NEOM & Giga Projects: $500B+ construction requiring specialist insurance — currently 100% broker/offline with no digital path
  • SME target: IA mandating SME insurance packages — digital delivery gap is policy priority; Vision 2030 targets 35% GDP from SMEs
  • Tourism: Saudi e-visa + tourism targets 150M visitors by 2030 — travel insurance demand surge; inbound coverage entirely undeveloped
  • Housing: 500K homes target — property/mortgage insurance entirely underdeveloped digitally
  • Sport & Events: Formula E, Dakar Rally, LIV Golf — event/liability insurance gap

🛡 Cyber Insurance: The SAR 2.4B Opportunity

  • NCA Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC) mandate for all entities — creates legal cyber insurance demand
  • PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law) fines up to SAR 5M — D&O and cyber liability need growing
  • Zero KSA insurers have retail cyber product online — first mover takes entire segment
  • Estimated KSA cyber GWP potential: SAR 2.4B (~$640M) by 2028 (internal estimate)
  • International players (Chubb, AIG) only serving large corporates via brokers — SME cyber is entirely open

⚖ Regulatory Catalysts

  • IA Open Insurance Framework: Consultation underway — API-based policy data sharing expected 2026–2027
  • SAMA Fintech Lab: Insurance sandbox graduates expected to challenge incumbents in 2026
  • Tameeni mandate: SAMA requires all motor policies issued via Tameeni — commoditises motor; shifts competition to health + life
  • CCHI health mandates: Expanding mandatory health to dependants — 3M+ new lives to insure
  • NCA ECC: Cybersecurity compliance forces cyber risk awareness across all enterprises — insurance demand follows

KSA market insight: Saudi Arabia is the GCC’s most digitally advanced insurance market yet paradoxically has the most untapped whitespace in high-value lines. Insurance revenue reached SAR 71.2B (~$19B) in 2025 (Milliman), growing at 7.3% YoY. Tawuniya and Bupa Arabia set the regional digital benchmark, but the entire market has zero cyber, minimal SME, and near-zero embedded insurance — precisely the segments that Vision 2030’s economic diversification agenda will drive demand for. The first carrier to launch a fully digital cyber + SME bundle will capture an estimated SAR 3B+ in addressable premium with almost no competition.
