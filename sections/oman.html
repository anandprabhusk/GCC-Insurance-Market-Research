Oman is the GCC’s least digitally mature insurance market, with ~$2.0B GWP in 2024 (CMA Oman Annual Report 2024 / AXCO), growing at 4.2% YoY. Regulated by the Capital Market Authority (CMA Oman), 21+ insurers serve a population of 4.5M with motor insurance the dominant line under mandatory TPL. Digital maturity stands at 43% (internal estimate) — lowest in GCC — with most carriers having basic web presence only. Sukoon Insurance leads as the clear frontrunner, but OQIC (Oman Qatar Insurance Company) has emerged as a strong #2 digital challenger following its 2023 online platform launch and acquisition of Vision Insurance. Oman Vision 2040’s economic diversification drive and mandatory health insurance expansion provide the structural demand catalysts, but digital insurance supply remains critically underdeveloped.

  • Tourism target: Oman targeting 11M tourists by 2040 — travel + hospitality insurance entirely underdeveloped
  • Free zones: Duqm, Sohar, Salalah free zones — FDI insurance + property + engineering demand growing
  • Dhamani mandatory health 2025: CMA expanding private health insurance mandate — 1.2M new policy holders; OQIC confirmed participant but no digital health journey
  • SME push: Oman targeting 20% SME GDP contribution — business interruption + property insurance demand emerging
  • National Cybersecurity Strategy 2024–2026: Government digital transformation driving corporate cyber risk; zero Oman carrier has any product

⚠ Oman-Specific Constraints

  • No cloud DC in Oman: CMA data residency requirements vs nearest AWS in Bahrain — compliance uncertainty for cloud migration
  • Legacy core systems: No standalone Oman carrier uses modern SaaS core — OQIC benefits from QIC Group shared core but others on legacy stacks
  • Market scale: ~$2.0B GWP limits tech investment ROI — OQIC’s QIC Group backing bypasses this constraint
  • Government health dominance: MOH covers ~56% Omanis; private health market limited to expats + private sector (Dhamani addresses this)
  • Bancassurance underdeveloped: Bank Muscat + NBO life sales dominant but no digital bancassurance journey
