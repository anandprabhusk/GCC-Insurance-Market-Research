📉 Growth Projections by Product (GCC Digital Premium 2026–2028) (Internal estimates)

🛡 Cyber — Highest growth vector

  • 2026: ~$10M digital premium (near-zero base)
  • 2027: ~$80M projected
  • 2028: ~$200M (350%+ CAGR from low base)
  • Driven by regional cyber mandate acceleration. First-mover will dominate a segment currently at near-zero digital distribution.

🩹 Health — Largest absolute opportunity

  • 2026: ~$3.6B digital premium
  • 2027: ~$5.2B projected
  • 2028: ~$7.0B
  • CCHI, DoH mandates and employer HR-tech integrations accelerating health digital onboarding at scale across KSA and UAE.

🚘 Motor — Proven, scalable, competitive

  • 2026: ~$5.2B digital premium
  • 2027: ~$6.7B projected
  • 2028: ~$8.3B
  • Still growing despite already being the most digitised line. Aggregators capture growing share; carriers must defend UX, pricing, and renewal flow.

Opportunity insight: The GCC insurance market reached $48.5B in 2025 (Alpen Capital, May 2026), growing at 4.9% annually toward $61.8B by 2030. Based on internal digital penetration modelling, an estimated $8–9B in additional digital premium is achievable by 2028 through direct and embedded channels. Cyber offers the highest percentage growth from near-zero base; health and motor offer the largest absolute dollar opportunities. UAE and KSA — each with ~$19–20.5B GWP — hold both the largest markets and the most untapped digital gaps in cyber, SME, home, and life. All digital projections in this section are internal estimates and should be treated as directional modelling, not official market forecasts.
