ℹ️ How to read this table — two critical definitions:
Digital Penetration % = the share of customers who used any digital channel during their insurance journey (online quote, comparison site, app, or digital renewal reminder). This is not the share of GWP transacted fully online — that figure is substantially lower, particularly for health, life, and complex commercial lines which remain broker-led across all GCC markets. Digital Maturity Score = a qualitative composite (0–5) assessed via public portal and app audits covering: self-service depth, mobile app quality, AI/chatbot presence, claims digitisation, and API openness. Scores carry ±1 point margin of error and reflect Q2 2026 observations only.

🔎 KSA vs UAE — why Advanced vs Leader despite KSA’s larger market?
KSA’s lower digital maturity score reflects its large mandatory health market, which remains employer/broker-contracted at scale, suppressing fully-digital transaction rates even though Tawuniya, Bupa Arabia, and ACIG operate GCC-leading apps with AI claims triage and telematics (Tawuniya Drive). UAE’s “Leader” status reflects both volume and the broadest online product range (cyber, SME, embedded), a mature aggregator ecosystem (Yallacompare, Policybazaar UAE), and the most active open-API / insurtech environment in the region. Technology Landscape Q2 2026 — Inferred
🏭 Cloud & Hosting

UAE leads with AWS and Azure multi-cloud deployments. KSA increasingly adopts the AWS Riyadh region (live since 2022) for data residency compliance. Qatar, Kuwait, and Oman largely rely on on-premise or regional data centres. Bahrain benefits from the AWS Bahrain region (live since 2019) — the first in the Middle East.

AWSAzureGoogle CloudOn-PremiseOracle Cloud
🛠 Core Insurance Platforms

Legacy COBOL/Oracle-based cores dominate older carriers. Newer entrants use Majesco, Guidewire, and Duck Creek. Anoud+ (by Anoud Technologies, founded by QIC with Swiss Re partnership) is the region’s most prominent GCC-native end-to-end core platform, deployed across multiple MENA carriers. SAP FS-CD is widely used by KSA government-linked insurers. Note: vendor attributions are inferred from public sources — not confirmed by carriers.

GuidewireMajescoDuck CreekAnoud+SAP FS-CDOracle
🤖 AI & Automation

Chatbots are common at UAE tier-1 carriers. KSA deploying NLP-based claims triage at Tawuniya and Bupa Arabia. Most GCC markets still use rule-based automation rather than true ML. Usage-based insurance (UBI) via telematics is growing in UAE — described as “becoming the main risk assessment tool” in 2025.
