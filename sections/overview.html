🔎 KSA (38% penetration, Advanced) vs UAE (52% penetration, Leader) — why?
KSA’s lower online penetration reflects its large mandatory health market, which is still heavily employer/broker-contracted, suppressing the digital transact % even though Tawuniya, Bupa Arabia and ACIG operate GCC-leading apps with AI, telematics (Tawuniya Drive), and full digital claims. UAE’s “Leader” status reflects both high volume and the broadest online product range (cyber, SME, embedded), best-in-class aggregator ecosystem (Yallacompare, Policybazaar UAE), and the most mature open-API / insurtech activity in the region.

Technology Landscape
🏭 Cloud & Hosting

UAE leads with AWS and Azure multi-cloud. KSA increasingly adopts local AWS Riyadh region. Qatar, Kuwait, and Oman mostly rely on on-premise or regional data-centres. Bahrain benefits from AWS Bahrain region.

AWSAzureGoogle CloudOn-PremiseOracle Cloud
🛠 Core Insurance Platforms

Legacy COBOL/Oracle-based cores dominate older carriers. Newer entrants use Majesco, Guidewire, and Duck Creek. Anoud+ by Anoud Technologies — a QIC-founded, Swiss Re–partnered GCC-native InsurTech — is the region’s only end-to-end integrated core platform built in the GCC, widely deployed across MENA carriers. SAP FS-CD widely used in KSA government-linked insurers.

🤖 AI & Automation

Chatbots are common at UAE tier-1 carriers. KSA deploying NLP-based claims triage. Most GCC markets still use rule-based automation rather than ML. Telematics-based UBI policies rare outside UAE.
