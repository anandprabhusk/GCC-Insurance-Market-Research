🇶🇦 Qatar — Insurance Market 2026
📋 Data source: Qatar total market GWP of approximately $2.4B (2026) is sourced from QCB Annual Report 2024 / AXCO Qatar Country Report 2025. Digital maturity score (64%) is an internal estimate based on product coverage and UX audits. Insurance is supervised by the Qatar Central Bank (QCB) under Law No. 13 of 2012. Tech stack data sourced from BuiltWith (qic.online), ZoomInfo / live HTTP headers (qiic.com.qa), and public signals (beema-online.com).

Qatar’s insurance market reached approximately $2.4B GWP in 2026, growing around 5% YoY, underpinned by mandatory motor and health schemes and a strong post-FIFA 2022 infrastructure base. The Qatar Central Bank (QCB) regulates the onshore sector under Law No. 13 of 2012, while the Qatar Financial Centre Regulatory Authority (QFCRA) separately regulates insurers operating within the QFC. QIC dominates with approximately 35% market share and the most advanced digital platform in the country. Most carriers offer basic online motor; deeper lines — cyber, life, SME — remain almost entirely offline.

📈 Qatar Market Intelligence — Unique Factors

🏜 FIFA 2022 Legacy & Infrastructure

  • Lusail + 7 stadia: ongoing operations require specialist liability + property insurance — all broker-only today
  • Hospitality boom: Qatar tourism target 6M visitors/year by 2030 — inbound travel insurance entirely underdeveloped digitally
  • Construction tail: Metro, Lusail City, Hamad Port — EPC + engineering insurance still 100% offline
  • National Vision 2030: economic diversification via QFC-registered SMEs — SME insurance gap = QAR 400M+ addressable

📶 Expat Market: 86% of Population

  • ~2.5M expats — mandatory health insurance (QCB / Supreme Health Council) creates large captive market
  • New-to-Qatar journey: health + motor + home all needed within 30 days — no digital bundle from any carrier
  • South Asian expat community (~55% of expats) — mobile-first, price-sensitive — underserved by Arabic/English-only platforms
  • High-income expat segment — life and savings insurance needs unmet digitally

⚖ Regulatory Catalysts (QCB / QFCRA)

  • QCB Digital Finance Strategy 2030: open banking + insurance API framework under development — first movers dominate embedded channel
  • QFC FinTech Hub: further insurtech entries expected 2026–2027; Beema QCB-licensed model is the template
  • Mandatory health expansion: QCB extending health mandate scope — adds 200K+ new insured lives
  • NCSA Cybersecurity Framework: Qatar NCSA mandates create latent corporate cyber insurance demand — Beema’s pilot is the only market response
